Ethical Guidelines and Policies for Research at IIT Delhi

Introduction

This document outlines the ethical guidelines and policies that must be adhered to by all researchers at IIT Delhi.

Research Integrity

All research conducted at IIT Delhi should be characterized by integrity and honesty. This includes a commitment to transparency, accuracy, and accountability in all phases of research.

Responsible Conduct of Research

Researchers are expected to engage in responsible conduct of research. This entails responsible collaboration, mentoring, authorship, and peer review.

Compliance with Regulations

Researchers must comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including but not limited to those relating to data protection, health and safety, and animal welfare.

Respect for Individuals and Communities

All research must respect the dignity, rights, and welfare of individuals and communities involved in research. Informed consent must be obtained when conducting research involving human subjects.

Accountability

Researchers are accountable for their research methods and findings. This includes the obligation to correct any errors and to promote accuracy in the dissemination of research results.

Environmental Responsibility

Researchers must consider the environmental impacts of their research and strive to minimize any negative effects on the environment.

Conclusion

Adhering to these ethical guidelines is crucial in fostering a culture of ethical research at IIT Delhi.
